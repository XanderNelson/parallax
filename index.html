FORD GT40

A REVOLUTION IN AMERICAN MUSCLE

MARK ONE

1964

The original Ford GT. In an attempt to defeat Ferrari at the 24 Hours of Le Mans, Ford began prototyping a new race car. The Mark I was initially unsuccessful, but after numerous modifications from the team at Shelby, Ford began recieving their first wins throughout the United States.

MARK TWO

1966

Similar in shape and size to the Mk. I, the Mk. II had accomodations for a larger, heavier engine. The Shelby redesign led to a much faster, more dominant car. The Mk. II blew away its competition at Le Mans, beating Ferrari in the famous first, second, third place win in 1966. It went on to win the next three years.

MARK FOUR

1967

The GT40 Mk. IV was a radical variant of the past models. A more aerodynamic shape and reinforced chassis gave the Mk. II an incredible top speed of 210 miles per hour. It only ran two races, the 1967 12 Hours of Sebring and 1967 24 Hours of Le Mans and took the victory at both races.
